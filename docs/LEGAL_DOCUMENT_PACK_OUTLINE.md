# Client Room Legal Document Pack Outline

This is the next layer to draft after approving the workflow and UI.

## 1. Preliminary Intake Notice

Purpose: protect the firm before a lawyer-client relationship begins.

Must state:

- submission is preliminary;
- no automatic engagement;
- no obligation to act;
- no filing until accepted;
- urgent matters may require direct contact or physical attendance.

## 2. Conflict Check Form

Must collect:

- client name;
- opposing parties;
- companies/institutions;
- police stations/courts/agencies;
- family members and agents;
- prior lawyers or existing representation.

## 3. Consent to Review

Must authorise the office to review submitted facts and documents for preliminary assessment.

## 4. AI-Assisted Organisation Notice

Must state:

- AI may assist transcription, chronology, document classification, and question generation;
- AI does not provide legal advice;
- final review and judgment remain with the lawyer.

## 5. Privacy Notice

Must explain:

- what data is collected;
- why it is collected;
- who may access it;
- how long it may be stored;
- client rights;
- security measures;
- third-party service providers.

## 6. Consultation / Intake Review Fee Notice

Must distinguish:

- consultation fee;
- professional fee;
- filing expenses;
- disbursement deposits.

## 7. Engagement Letter

Must cover:

- scope of work;
- exclusions;
- professional fees;
- filing expenses;
- client duties;
- reporting;
- confidentiality;
- termination;
- limitation of representation;
- dispute resolution.

## 8. Filing Instruction and Expense Deposit Form

Used only after engagement and lawyer confirmation.

Must cover:

- court/registry/agency;
- process to be filed;
- estimated filing expense;
- professional fee balance;
- client authority to proceed;
- accounting for disbursements.

## 9. Non-Engagement / Decline Letter

For matters the firm cannot accept.

Must avoid giving detailed advice unless consultation occurred.

## 10. Existing Client Room Terms

Must cover:

- matter-code use;
- confidentiality;
- document upload responsibility;
- no sharing of login credentials;
- office response windows;
- communication boundaries.
